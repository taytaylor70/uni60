# The Taylor Dynasty Holding — Site Strategy Blueprint

> Privately curated digital assets, built like luxury.
> Every product is engineered for immediate, professional-grade execution.

---

## The 5 Pages You Actually Need

---

### Page 1 — Homepage
**URL:** `/`
**Goal:** Establish immediate category authority. A visitor must know in under 10 seconds who this is, what's built here, and why it belongs in a different tier than everything else. Luxury positioning is the only impression that matters.

**Single Action Every Page Pushes Toward:**
→ **"Explore the Portfolio"** — route qualified visitors to the asset catalog, nothing else.

**What lives here:**
- Manifesto headline (one sentence, no hedging)
- The 3 flagship assets surfaced as cards — each with a single outcome sentence
- One proof paragraph: who builds here, what they've shipped, why this is different
- Zero fluff. No testimonials yet. No explainer sections.

---

### Page 2 — Portfolio (The Asset Catalog)
**URL:** `/portfolio`
**Goal:** Make each digital product feel like a collectible object, not a SaaS listing. Each asset has a name, an outcome, a spec sheet, and an access tier. Visitors see the full range; the depth of the catalog signals enterprise seriousness.

**Single Action Every Page Pushes Toward:**
→ **"Acquire Access"** — one button per asset, always. No pricing menus. No free trial language. Access is requested, not subscribed to.

**What lives here:**
- Grid of assets — each with: name, tagline, status (live / in development), access type
- Current flagship: HEXAGEN-60 (uni60) — hierarchical Unicode Base-60 ID protocol
- Each card links to a dedicated product page (not a modal, a full page)

---

### Page 3 — About (The Architecture)
**URL:** `/about`
**Goal:** Build credibility through depth, not biography. The reader should leave understanding the founding philosophy — that Taylor Dynasty builds the primitives other systems run on — and trust that every product is engineered, not assembled.

**Single Action Every Page Pushes Toward:**
→ **"Read the Manifesto"** — anchor link to a long-form philosophy statement at the bottom of the page.

**What lives here:**
- The holding company structure: Taylor Dynasty Holding → subsidiaries
- The operating thesis: "We don't ship features. We ship infrastructure."
- Current subsidiary spotlight: Taylor Dynasty Systems (HEXAGEN-60 / uni60)
- No founder photos. No LinkedIn plugs. The work is the biography.

---

### Page 4 — Acquire (Access & Licensing)
**URL:** `/acquire`
**Goal:** Convert intent into revenue. This page exists for one type of visitor: someone ready to deploy. It has no educating to do — that happened on Portfolio. It presents two or three clear access tiers with zero ambiguity about what each includes.

**Single Action Every Page Pushes Toward:**
→ **"Request Access"** — a short form (name, org, use case, tier). Not a checkout flow. A request. Scarcity and curation are part of the brand.

**What lives here:**
- Three tiers: Open (MIT/free), Professional (license + support), Enterprise (custom)
- For each tier: what's included, what's explicitly not included, one action
- A one-sentence qualification statement: "Access is extended to teams building production infrastructure."

---

### Page 5 — Intelligence (The Brief)
**URL:** `/intelligence`
**Goal:** Authority through output. Long-form technical writing, protocol analysis, and engineering philosophy that demonstrates Taylor Dynasty Systems thinks at a level above the competition. This page feeds SEO and converts readers into buyers over time.

**Single Action Every Page Pushes Toward:**
→ **"Subscribe to The Brief"** — email capture. One field. No offers, no lead magnets. The Brief is the offer.

**What lives here:**
- Published articles: technical depth, no filler, no listicles
- Category: Distributed Systems, ID Architecture, Protocol Design, Engineering Philosophy
- Tone: Peer-to-peer. Written for senior engineers, not managers.

---

## Brand Constraints (Apply to All 5 Pages)

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| Color | Black (#0A0A0A), Gold (#C9A84C), Cream (#F5F0E8) only |
| Typography | Playfair Display (headings) + Inter (body) |
| CTA language | Never "Sign up", "Try it", "Learn more" — always active, outcome-specific |
| Tone | Authoritative, minimal, peer-level. No hype. No startup-speak. |
| Images | Code, architecture diagrams, or nothing. No stock photography. |
| Navigation | Logo + 4 items + one outlined action button. That's it. |
